Moroccan Burgers

A Moroccan twist on a classic beef burger

  1. Home
  2. Cooking
  3. Moroccan Burgers
  • Serves: 4
  • Cooking Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients:

  • Olive oil
  • 1 red onion, thinliy sliced into half moons
  • Potatoes, cut into wedges
  • 1.5tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 500g minced beef
  • 1.5tsp ras-el-hanout
  • 4 bread buns

Method:

  1. Preheat the oven to 200Â°C. Lay the potato wedges on a cooking tray, drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper. Toss to coat. Cook in the oven for 30-35min, shaking the tray halfway through.
  2. Put a drizzle of oil olive in a small sauceplan on a low heat. Add the onion, balsamic vinegar and season. Place a lid on the pan and cook gently for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. In a mixing bowl, combine the mince with the ras-el-hanout and a pinch of salt. Once combined, press the mince together to create burger patties. Don't press too firmly as this can make the burger tough.
  4. Put a splash of oil in a frying pan on a medium heat. Once hot add the burgers and cook on each side for 5-6 minutes.
  5. Serve the burgers in the buns with a spoonful of the red onion relish and a side of wedges.
